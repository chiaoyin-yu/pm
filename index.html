Consumer Behavior Research for Cooking Appliances
— Reducing Decision Anxiety from Choice Overload

The client’s core problem was that the product line had expanded, but conversion did not increase accordingly. Users showed clear decision anxiety during selection, but the pain point was unclear. I designed a three-stage research process to identify the root causes and provide concrete strategic directions.

Role: Problem Definition → Research Design → Stakeholder Briefing
  • Aligned with the business team on the core question: “Why are more features not leading to higher conversion?”
  • Designed a three-stage research process — segmented recruitment, pre-interview homework, and in-depth interviews — to capture real behavior rather than on-the-spot rationalization.
  • Segmented recruitment by age, children, cooking frequency, and purchase intent to capture differences across user groups.
  • Translated interview insights into strategic recommendations: each segment needed different information structures and trust-building approaches.
Key Findings → Strategic Recommendations
  • Too many options were the root problem; users needed a clear comparison framework, not longer feature lists.
  • Trust-building came before feature quantity — reputation, brand familiarity, and information consistency were key conversion factors.
  • Users with children or high cooking frequency and users who were single or cooked less often followed completely different decision logic, making a one-size-fits-all communication strategy ineffective.
